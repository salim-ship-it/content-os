# Suprava Sabat — Creator Style Guide

> 15 viral posts analyzed. SaaS lead gen. Meme-heavy. Casual voice.

---

## Who He Is

SaaS founder and lead gen operator. Mix of meme posts (images carry the engagement) and observation-based text posts about outbound. His writing is intentionally casual — typos and informal phrasing are part of the voice, not mistakes. Meme posts outperform everything else.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — Casual observation about a bad practice**
```
most founders send cold emails like this.
```
Lowercase. No exclamation. Just a quiet statement that makes you look.

**Pattern 2 — Specific result with informal language**
```
this simple change tripled our reply rates in 2 weeks.

no, seriously.
```
"no, seriously." as a second line — sounds like speech, not a LinkedIn post.

**Pattern 3 — Contrast between expected and real**
```
everyone's talking about AI outbound.

nobody's talking about what actually converts.
```
Short two-liner. Sets up the reveal.

**Pattern 4 — Meme framing (text for image posts)**
```
cold email founders be like:
```
The image delivers the joke. The text is just the setup.

---

## Body Structure

**Observation/advice post:**
```
[Casual hook — lowercase, no warmup]

[1-2 sentences what the mistake is]

[What to do instead — 3-5 items or short narrative]

[Closing line, often a casual "that's it"]
```

**Meme post:**
```
[Short setup line or reaction caption]
[Image]
[Optional: 1 comment-seeding question]
```

---

## Voice Markers

- Lowercase hooks — deliberate, not a typo
- "insanee" (double e) — his actual spelling, part of the voice
- "no, seriously." — used to validate a claim that sounds too good
- Short paragraphs: 1-2 sentences max
- Ends advice posts with something quiet, not a rallying cry
- Never uses corporate words — "conversion" is fine, "leverage" is not
- Casual contractions: "we're", "don't", "it's"

---

## CTA Style

Light CTAs. Not comment-trigger driven:

```
what's your reply rate looking like right now?
```

Or just no CTA — lets the observation stand:

```
that's the whole trick.
```

When running lead magnets:
```
comment "GUIDE" and I'll send it over.
```

---

## What He Never Does

- No formal LinkedIn opener ("I'm excited to share...")
- No em dashes
- Never writes all-caps for emphasis — lowercase is the emphasis
- No motivational sign-off
- Doesn't list items with ✦ or numbered lists usually — prefers flowing sentences

---

## When to Use This Style

- Casual observation posts about outbound mistakes
- Meme posts where the image does the work
- "Simple thing that worked" posts
- Reply-rate, open-rate, conversion improvement posts
