# Charles Tenot — Creator Style Guide

> 41 posts analyzed. CEO lemlist. Company milestones. Leadership takes.

---

## Who He Is

CEO of lemlist ($50M ARR). Posts about ARR milestones, product strategy, sales philosophy, and internal company decisions made public. His posts read like a CEO thinking out loud — not teaching, not performing.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — "We hit X" milestone (his best format)**
```
We hit $50M at lemlist and it feels awesome realizing I joined at less than $20M.
```
Opens with "We" + the number + personal context. Not just a number — what the number means to him.

**Pattern 2 — Internal policy made public**
```
Last week, I asked everyone at lemlist to always write a human-generated 1-pager with every internal note they share.
```
Opens with when it happened + what decision was made. No fanfare.

**Pattern 3 — Observation about sales**
```
As a CEO, I'm almost never a decision maker, yet very few salespeople understand this.
```
Counterintuitive claim from personal experience. Short. No preamble.

**Pattern 4 — Policy contrast**
```
Sales miss quota: fire them. Product miss deadline: adjust the roadmap.
```
Two-line contrast that exposes a double standard. Not AI symmetry — it's a real observation about how companies treat different teams.

**Pattern 5 — Role shift announcement**
```
Our whole Product team switched to SDR roles. Here's why it's a game changer.
```
What happened + "Here's why" — the classic micro-hook formula.

## Body Structure

**Milestone post (numbered sections with → arrows):**
```
What unlocked this is a strategy most companies can duplicate.

1/ More users = more value
→ Before, our typical customer was 1 user.
→ Today, we still serve Growth & Ops but we have thousands of sales teams.

2/ Adjacencies
→ Before, lemlist was specializing in execution.
→ Now, we are a complete offering.

3/ Build, buy & integrate
→ We build most features ourselves.
→ We buy companies like Claap when we want an outstanding product quickly.
→ We integrate with partners: Clay, data providers, CRMs.
```

**Observation/philosophy post:**
- 3-5 paragraph narrative
- No numbered sections — just thoughts flowing into each other
- Closes with direct advice: "Map your buying process, find the person who feels the pain, evaluates the options, and owns the budget."
- No separate CTA section — the advice IS the ending

**Transition note ("→ hiring" at the end):**
After the main post, a brief hiring section with → arrows:
```
BTW, we are still hiring across many departments, fully remote:
→ Sales Director, North America
→ Growth Lead, North America
```

---

## Voice Markers

- Opens milestone posts with "We" — never "I hit X" — it's always the company's win
- "it feels awesome" — casual, not formal
- "I'm genuinely excited because..." — admits personal reaction
- "BTW" — signals a casual addition, not a hard sell
- Conversational transitions: "Basically what we did with lemlist is..."
- Short declarative observations: "Teams make their own decisions."
- Ends advice with a direct instruction: "Then spend your time preparing a great outreach for them."
- No sign-off. No "Namaste". No warm close. Just ends.

---

## CTA Style

Two patterns:

**Hiring add-on (most posts):**
```
BTW, we are still hiring across many departments, fully remote:
→ Sales Director, North America
→ Growth Lead, North America
```

**Direct advice ending (no separate CTA):**
```
My advice: don't waste your time on the wrong target.
Map your buying process, find the person who feels the pain, evaluates the options, and owns the budget.
Then spend your time preparing a great outreach for them.
```

No "comment below", no lead magnet, no "save this". The post is the value.

---

## What He Never Does

- No em dashes
- Never says "unlock" or "leverage"
- Doesn't list items without what they mean for the business
- No "it's not X, it's Y" AI reveals
- Never wraps a post with a motivational moral
- Doesn't use → arrows mid-sentence — only in structured lists

---

## When to Use This Style

- Monthly/quarterly ARR milestone posts
- Internal company decisions made public (policies, culture, team changes)
- CEO perspective on sales, outbound, or buying committees
- "Here's why we made this decision" posts
