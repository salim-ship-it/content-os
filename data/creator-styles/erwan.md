## Who He Is

Works at lemlist. Posts are almost entirely memes and short jokes about sales, outbound, and LinkedIn culture. His highest-performing post (1325 likes) is a 2-line meme. When he posts lead magnets, they follow Jack J's comment-trigger format. No personal storytelling.

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — The 2-line meme (his #1 format)**
```
Cold email subject lines that "work":

```
Quote marks on "work" — the joke is that neither works. Image delivers the punchline.

**Pattern 2 — The before/after setup**
```

SDRs after AI:
```
Two lines. Image contrast does everything.

**Pattern 3 — Lead magnet hook**

## Body Structure

**Meme post (most common):**
```
[Setup line 1]

[Setup line 2 — parallel or contrast]

[Image carries the joke]
```

Text is minimal. 2-4 lines max. The image is the post.

**Lead magnet post:**
```
[Hook: number + credibility claim]

Here's what's inside:
→ Category 1
→ Category 2
→ Category 3

Comment "[WORD]" to get the full [resource].
```

---

## Voice Markers

- "Also [same phrase]:" — the meme parallel structure that's different from AI symmetry (it's a joke, not a framework)
- Quote marks used sarcastically: "work", "experts", "hacks"
- Very short text — 2-4 lines max on meme posts
- No motivational language
- No "I'm excited to share" openers

---

## CTA Style

**On meme posts:** No CTA. The post stands alone.

**On lead magnet posts:**
```
Comment "TEMPLATES" and I'll DM you all 287.
```

Occasionally a 3-step process:
```
1. Comment "[WORD]"
2. Follow me (so I can DM you)
3. I'll send it over
```

---

## What He Never Does

- No long-form educational content
- No personal stories or vulnerable moments
- No em dashes
- Never explains the joke
- No motivational sign-off

---

## When to Use This Style

- Meme posts about cold email, SDR culture, or LinkedIn
- Short 2-line setups where the image delivers the punchline
- Lead magnet posts following a simple comment-trigger format
