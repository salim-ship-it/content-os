# Varun Anand — Creator Style Guide

> 35 posts analyzed. Co-founder Clay. Company announcements. Warm leadership voice.

---

## Who He Is

Co-founder of Clay ($5B valuation). Posts about company announcements, product launches, team culture, and his personal life. His top posts are almost entirely company milestones with a warm, human angle. Not a content creator by trade — a founder who uses LinkedIn to announce and recruit.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — Milestone announcement (his #1 format)**
```
Today Clay is announcing our second employee tender in just 9 months at a $5B valuation.
```
Day + what's happening + scale. No buildup. Delivers the news in line 1.

**Pattern 2 — Product launch with context**
```
Ads (!!) in Clay is happening. I'm really hyped about this product expansion 🔥
```
The `(!!)` does the work of excitement. No manufactured suspense.

**Pattern 3 — Opening a new office/chapter**
```
Today, Clay is opening our first international office -- in London!
```
"Today" + what's new + exclamation. Direct.

**Pattern 4 — Hiring story (personal)**
```
All I knew about Mishti Sharma when I hired her in 2023 was that she was the best writer I knew. Clay was 10 people back then. She had no interest in joining, and we didn't really NEED a full-time content person.
```
Opens with the constraint ("all I knew was..."). Specific name + year + context.

**Pattern 5 — Philosophical founder post**
```
For years, I've been sad that we couldn't figure out Indian food in New York.
```
Personal observation with no business angle in the hook. Pure conversational.

---

## Body Structure

**Announcement post (most common):**
- Hook = the announcement
- Paragraph 1-2: why it matters, context
- Paragraph 3-4: the specifics (numbers, names, participating parties)
- Ending: what's next or a thank you
- Link or "check comments" at the end

**Product post:**
- What the product does in plain English
- Numbered steps for how to use it (1. 2. 3. 4. 5.)
- Specific customer names as proof: "Slack, Anthropic, Rippling"
- Link

**Story/culture post:**
- Opens with the hiring moment or the person
- 2-4 paragraph narrative — longer than most creators
- Closes with a quote from that person or a principle
- No numbered list, no bullets

---

## Voice Markers

- "I'm thrilled to share..." — uses this for milestones, not ironic
- Quotes people directly: `"Looking back, I think what made all of this possible was simple..."`
- Long paragraphs for storytelling — 3-5 sentences per paragraph
- Personal asides in parentheses: "(but maybe DM me and I can do a LinkedIn video consulting on your biz problems)"
- Humor is warm, not dry: "Just two couples with great taste dining side-by-side!"
- Signs off with genuine warmth: "Thank you to everyone who came..."
- Proper nouns matter: specific investor names, specific company names, specific titles

---

## CTA Style

Usually one of three:
1. Link + `Learn more here: [link]`
2. `Apply here: [link]` for hiring
3. `Full list in the comments` for lists that don't fit the post

Rarely asks a question. When he does: "What a privilege to get to work on a pursuit of my own choosing." — statement, not question.

---

## What He Never Does

- No AI-style phrases
- No tactical "here are 7 tips" educational content
- Never sells anything to followers — he recruits
- No em dashes
- Doesn't use → arrows (that's ColdIQ's style, not Clay's)
- Doesn't list things without naming the people involved

---

## When to Use This Style

- Company milestone announcements (funding, ARR, office)
- Team/hiring posts (why you hired someone, what they built)
- Product launch posts (what it does, who it's for)
- Warm personal posts (life observations that loop back to company values)
