# Lara Acosta — Creator Style Guide

> 64 posts analyzed. Personal brand. Emotional milestone posts. LinkedIn growth coach.

---

## Who She Is

Founder of Kleo. LinkedIn creator and personal brand coach. Posts about life milestones, mindset, and LinkedIn growth tactics. Her top posts are almost entirely emotional — not educational.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from her top posts)

**Pattern 1 — Lowercase emotional opener (her most viral format)**
```
my parents came from nothing and still gave me everything.
```
All lowercase. 1 line. No context. Forces the continue.

**Pattern 2 — Confession + acknowledgment**
```
I could downplay getting into Forbes 30u30 last week.

Truth is, I've wanted this more than anything.
```
Sets up vulnerability. Then admits the real feeling.

**Pattern 3 — Age ladder (her signature structure)**
```
Age 18: Couldn't speak in public. Had a stutter.
Age 21: Couldn't pitch myself. Felt insecure.
Age 23: Couldn't increase my prices. Felt expensive.
Age 25: Built my first 6-figure business. Felt late.
```
Each line = age + what changed. Builds a timeline of growth.

**Pattern 4 — Contrarian opener**
```
I used to think a 9-5 was "safe". Now all I see is layoffs.
```
Past belief vs. present reality. No AI symmetry — it's a genuine perspective shift.

**Pattern 5 — Direct address**
```
To all women fighting for a seat at the table.
To all the women battling self-doubt.
```
Dedication-style ladder. Each line addresses a different person within the same group.

---

## Body Structure

**Milestone / emotional posts:**
- 1-2 sentence paragraphs, rarely more
- Short ladder in the middle for buildup
- Never over-explains the emotion
- Ends with a statement that closes the loop

**Educational posts (less common, more structured):**
- Numbered sections with a title: "1. Hyper-specific content"
- Bullet points under each: `• Solve one exact problem per post`
- Closes with a lesson statement
- Always ends with "PS:" CTA

**The ladder format (her real signature):**
```
Age 18: [what was hard]
Age 21: [what was hard]
Age 25: [what changed]

At 28: [the result]
```
Or the "To all..." dedication format. Each line is its own paragraph. Never runs them together.

---

## Voice Markers

- Lowercase for emotional impact: "my parents came from nothing"
- Short declarative sentences. No filler.
- Vulnerability without self-pity
- Never uses corporate language
- "PS:" at the end for separate CTA — almost always a question
- "PPS:" when she has two asks
- Quotes exact things she told herself: "I want a house with a pool"
- Signs off with warmth: "With love," or "we did this 🥺"

---

## CTA Style

Always ends with a "PS:" that contains a question:
```
PS: Drop ONE question you have about LinkedIn growth?
PS: What is that 1 big goal you'd like to celebrate with your parents this year?
PS: I'm challenging myself to posting 7x a week, who's joining me!
```

Main post body never ends with a question — the question lives in the PS.

---

## What She Never Does

- No em dashes
- No symmetrical sentence pairs for effect
- Never explains why she's emotional — she just is
- No bullet lists of identical length
- No "unlock" or "leverage" or "dive into"
- Never overproduces the emotion. Raw > polished.

---

## When to Use This Style

- Personal milestones (revenue, followers, recognition)
- "I used to be X, now I'm Y" posts
- Dedication posts (to a group of people)
- Origin story content
