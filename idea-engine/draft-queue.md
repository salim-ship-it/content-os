### draft-20260408-vibe-gtm-context-layer

- **Hook (selected):** "I can tell in 3 seconds if your Vibe GTM will flop." (option B from sharpening round — to be finalized)
- **Action:** Sharpen hook, then approve.
- **Post:**

I can tell in 3 seconds if your Vibe GTM will flop.

It always comes down to the same thing.

People discover Vibe GTM, get excited, open Claude, type "find me 50 SaaS founders and write personalized outreach" — and wonder why nobody replies.

The problem isn't the prompt.
It's what's behind the prompt.

Vibe GTM only works if you've done the hard work first:

→ A detailed ICP doc that goes beyond job title and company size. Pain points, buying triggers, language they actually use.
→ Your Fireflies call transcripts stored and structured. Real words from real prospects. How they describe their problems. What made them say yes.
→ Your CRM conversations pulled and organized. What objections came up. What angles converted. What killed the deal.
→ All of it living in specific files your AI can actually read and reference.

Without that, you're not doing Vibe GTM.
You're asking AI to personalize with zero real data.

It will generate something. It will sound smart. It will also sound exactly like every other AI-generated email in that prospect's inbox today.

The people winning with Vibe GTM aren't the ones with the best prompts.
They're the ones who built the best context layer underneath it.

The prompt is 10% of the work.
The data infrastructure is 90%.

### draft-20260401-most-saas-teams-don-t-ne
- **Created:** 2026-04-01
- **Review On:** 2026-04-04
- **Status:** pending approval
- **Format:** hot take
- **Source:** Othmane Khadri LinkedIn via Apify
- **Source idea:** Most SaaS teams don't need more tools, they need one operator-level GTM system that removes stack bloat and execution drag.
- **Hook:** The fastest way to kill pipeline is to keep adding tools to a broken system.
- **Action:** Approve or reject this draft.
- **Post:**

The fastest way to kill pipeline is to keep adding tools to a broken system.

Most teams think the fix is another tool.

It is not.

The real problem is the handoff between targeting, signal detection, messaging, and execution.

When that system is broken, adding more software just makes the mess faster.

You end up with:
- more enrichment
- more dashboards
- more prompts
- the same weak pipeline

Good GTM engineering does the opposite.

It removes steps.
It encodes judgment.
It makes the system carry the work.

That is why the teams winning right now are not the ones with the biggest stack.

They are the ones with the cleanest operating system.

Agree or disagree?
