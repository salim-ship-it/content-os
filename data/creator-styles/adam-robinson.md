# Adam Robinson — Creator Style Guide

> 58 posts analyzed. Bootstrapped SaaS founder. Contrarian. Story-first.

---

## Who He Is

Co-founder/CEO of RB2B ($8M ARR). Posts about bootstrapping, founder mindset, AI, and the reality of building without VC. His posts read like founder conversations — long, specific, opinionated.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — Quote or scene opener (his best format)**
```
After the founder of Twitch (Justin Kan) sold his company to Amazon for $1 billion, an interviewer asked him, "Don't you think you won the lottery?" His answer was the best description of being a founder I've ever heard:
```
Opens in the middle of a real story. Credits the source. Long hook — but earns the click.

**Pattern 2 — Specific scene + vulnerability**
```
Last week I was at an 8-person founder breakfast in Aspen. Guy goes around the table and asks everyone how they're using AI day-to-day. I was super embarrassed to answer the question, but I did.
```
Time + place + what happened + emotional state. All in the hook.

**Pattern 3 — Third party proof**
```
Taylor Haren was Clay's largest user at one point, hitting their platform 17.3 million times per week. Last month he replaced it with a $200/mo Claude Code subscription.
```
Specific name + specific number + surprising result.

**Pattern 4 — Direct statement with challenge**
```
I want to be crystal clear: the advice I give on LinkedIn is NOT for founders who think they're creating unicorns, decacorns, or the next Anthropic.
```
Sets a boundary. Creates in-group vs. out-group. Earns respect from the right readers.

**Pattern 5 — Announcement with context**
```
I'm THRILLED to announce that RB2B just crossed $8m ARR! Last year we let AI run RB2B for 7 days. This year we're going to make that 7 months!
```
Milestone + "here's what makes this interesting" in the same line.

---

## Body Structure

**Story post (most common):**
- Opens with a scene
- Builds to an insight or turning point
- Draws a conclusion from the real event
- Short numbered list if it's a "here's what I learned" post
- Closes with a question or provocative statement

**Lesson/framework post:**
- Opens with a strong claim
- Numbered list (1. 2. 3.)
- Each point is 2-4 sentences — not just a header
- Closes with an invitation ("Can we do it? LET ME KNOW IN THE COMMENTS")

**Conversational paragraphs — his real pattern:**
```
He said, "Want to know something crazy? [quote]"

I've never had a $1b exit.

But I've been in this startup-game long enough to know he's right.

Every company you start is a ticket.
Every failure is a ticket.
Every painful lesson is a ticket.
```
Medium-length paragraphs. Then short punchy lines at the end. Rhythm changes.

---

## Voice Markers

- Uses real names, real events, real numbers
- Calls out his own embarrassment or failure directly: "I was super embarrassed"
- All caps for emphasis: "FREEDOM", "NOT for founders", "LET ME KNOW"
- "..." used for conversational pauses
- "So I ask people why they have them:" — colon as a natural pause
- Ends with questions: "Thoughts?", "Can we do it?", "...what did I miss?"
- Never performs humility — admits weakness and moves on

---

## CTA Style

Often a direct question to the comments:
```
Thoughts?
Can we do it? LET ME KNOW IN THE COMMENTS.
…what did I miss?
Did I miss anything in my analysis???
```

Or a challenge/invitation:
```
Come join the company that's charting the future of GTM
```

---

## What He Never Does

- No em dashes
- No "it's not X, it's Y" — he just states his actual view
- No neat moral wrapping at the end
- No parallel bullets where every line is the same length
- Never preachy. Makes his point and leaves space.

---

## When to Use This Style

- Sharing a story from a real event (dinner, meeting, breakfast)
- Third-party proof posts (someone else did something interesting)
- Contrarian takes with a personal stake in the outcome
- Milestone posts with a surprising angle
