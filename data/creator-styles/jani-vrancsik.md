# Jani Vrancsik — Creator Style Guide

> 3 viral posts analyzed. Resource compilation. Hierarchy lists. Generous framing.

---

## Who He Is

Curator and resource compiler. Posts free resource roundups for sales and outbound people. His posts are long, nested lists — main categories with sub-items. The "I spent X hours collecting every Y so you don't have to" framing is his signature. Low post volume but high engagement when he does publish.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — "I spent X collecting Y so you don't have to"**
```
I spent 40+ hours collecting every cold email resource so you don't have to.
```
Time invested + generous transfer. The "so you don't have to" is doing the work.

**Pattern 2 — Number + type of resource**
```
63 free cold outreach tools in one post.
```
Raw count + "in one post" = everything you need, right here.

**Pattern 3 — The summary before the list**
```
I put together the most complete B2B lead gen resource list I've ever seen.

Here's what's inside:
```
Credibility claim + "Here's what's inside" bridge.

---

## Body Structure

**The nested hierarchy list (his core format):**
```
[Hook]

[1-line bridge: "Here's everything:"]

→ Category 1
   ↳ Sub-item with what it does
   ↳ Sub-item with what it does

→ Category 2
   ↳ Sub-item
   ↳ Sub-item

→ Category 3
   ↳ Sub-item

[Repost line or follow CTA]
```

**The ↳ arrow for sub-items is his signature** — nested → then ↳ for the items inside each category.

---

## Voice Markers

- → for categories, ↳ for sub-items
- "so you don't have to" — almost always present in the hook
- Time investment upfront: "40+ hours", "3 months"
- "most complete" — confidence in the compilation
- "Here's what's inside:" as the bridge line
- No personal story or context — straight to the list

---

## CTA Style

**Repost ask:**
```
Repost ♻️ so this reaches someone who needs it.
```

**Follow CTA:**
```
Follow me for weekly resource roundups.
```

Sometimes both. No comment-trigger CTAs in his viral posts.

---

## What He Never Does

- No personal stories
- No hot takes or opinions
- No em dashes
- No "here's my framework" — only curated resources
- Never explains why a resource is good — just names and what it does

---

## When to Use This Style

- Free resource compilation posts
- Tool roundup posts
- "Everything in one place" posts with nested categories
- Posts where the value IS the comprehensive list
