## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — Dialogue opener (his #1 viral format)**
```
"Mom how did we get so poor?"

"Well sweetie, your dad decided to vibe code his CRM instead of improving his own product…"
```
The setup is 5 words. The punchline lands in the reply.

**Pattern 2 — Absurd premise stated as fact**
```
I've cold emailed Elon musk 27 times.
```
No explanation. No context. Forces the read-more.

**Pattern 3 — Build update with a number**
```
30 days ago i started a challenge to rebuild Clay as an open source GTM operating system from scratch. Today is day 30 and i did it.
```
Milestone + tension. Specific timeframe. Past tense.

**Pattern 4 — Satirical blessing post**
```
May 2026 bring profitability to series B companies.
May 2026 bless SDRs teams with better managers.
```
Parallel structure used for COMEDY only. Each line is a punchline.

**Pattern 5 — Price shock**
```
Clay Pro costs $720/month. That's $8,640/year.

But what if it was free?
```
Specific number → contrast.

---

## Body Structure

**Short build posts (most common):**
- 1-3 sentence paragraphs
- No numbered sections
- Dry punchline lands at the end
- The joke is the point — no lesson extracted

**Long breakdown posts:**
- Numbered list (1) 2) 3))
- Each item = 1-3 lines, specific
- No bullets inside items
- Ends with the actual call to action (link or DM)

**Build diary format:**
```
Day X/30. [what happened]

[1-3 lines on what was built or learned]

[What's next or honest reflection]
```

---

## Voice Markers

- Lowercase for casual: "i did it", "i'd be a mediocre CEO"
- No AI polish. Rough edges intentional.
- Sarcasm delivered deadpan
- Self-deprecating: "I'm still a little stunned typing that"
- Ends jokes without explaining them
- Signs off with his brand name only when relevant: "Earleads(.)com can do that for you ;)"
- Winky face or nothing — no filler emojis

---

## What He Never Does

- No em dashes
- No "it's not X, it's Y" reveals
- No motivational landing statements
- No parallel bullets that all rhyme or match length
- Never explains a joke
- No "great news" or "I'm excited to announce" without a number behind it

---

## When to Use This Style

- Sarcastic takes on the GTM space
- Build-in-public updates (day X of Y)
- Contrarian takes on tools (Clay, AI, SDRs)
- Short punchy posts where the hook IS the point
