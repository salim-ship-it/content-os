# Charlie Hills — Creator Style Guide

> 50 viral posts analyzed. B2B sales resources. List-format content. Massive reach.

---

## Who He Is

B2B sales resource curator. Posts almost exclusively about free resources, tools, and lists for SDRs and sales reps. His content is not opinion — it's curation. Every post is a packed list with a free download or link. Highest engagement in the batch: 2733 likes on a single post.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — Authority won't share this**
```
Your manager won't send you this list.
```
Short. Implies you're getting insider access. Creates instant "why not?" tension.

**Pattern 2 — You're doing something wrong by a deadline**
```
You are wasting 3 hours a day by not using these tools.
```
Direct accusation + time cost. No warmup. Hits immediately.

**Pattern 3 — Everything in one place**
```
47 B2B sales resources in one place.
```
The number + "in one place" is the entire hook. Volume = credibility.

**Pattern 4 — Most people stop at X**
```
85% of SDRs stop at 3 cold email subject lines.
```
Percentage + the mistake. You want to be in the 15%.

**Pattern 5 — Free resource announcement**
```
I've spent 3 years collecting the best cold outreach templates.

Saving them here so you don't have to.
```
Time invested + payoff for reader. Generous framing.

## Body Structure

**The standard list post:**
```
[Hook line]

[Short bridge: "Here's what's inside:" or "Everything you need:"]

✦ Resource 1 — what it does
✦ Resource 2 — what it does
✦ Resource 3 — what it does
[continues for 8-20 items]

[Free resource link or "Comment X to get the full list"]

Repost ♻️ to help someone in your network.

P.S. [A single question to drive comments]
```

**The numbered framework post:**
```
[Hook]

1. [Item]
2. [Item]
3. [Item]

[Closing line]
```

---

## Voice Markers

- ✦ bullet style — his signature, not dashes or arrows
- "Repost ♻️ to help someone in your network." — almost every post ends with this
- "P.S." — always followed by a question to seed comments
- No personal stories. Never "I felt..." or "This happened to me..."
- Very direct: "Here's what you need" not "Here are some thoughts"
- Uses exact numbers: "47 resources", "3 hours a day", "85% of SDRs"
- No adjectives on the resources themselves — just what they do

---

## CTA Style

Two standard endings, often combined:

**Repost ask:**
```
Repost ♻️ to help someone in your network.
```

**Comment trigger:**
```
Comment "LIST" and I'll DM you the full 47-resource breakdown.
```

**P.S. engagement question:**
```
P.S. What's the one resource you wish you'd found earlier in your career?
```

---

## What He Never Does

- No personal stories or vulnerable moments
- No opinions on industry trends
- No "hot takes" or contrarian positions
- Never uses em dashes
- Never writes philosophical closers
- No "unlock", "leverage", or AI buzzwords
- Doesn't explain his sources or methodology

---

## When to Use This Style

- Lead magnet posts (free resource offers)
- Tool/resource roundups
- "Everything in one place" list posts
- Posts where the value IS the list itself
