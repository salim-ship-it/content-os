# Jacob Pegs — Creator Style Guide

> 34 posts analyzed. Personal brand. Raw emotional posts. Anti-AI sarcasm.

---

## Who He Is

Coach and creator. Posts about money, family, AI (mostly to mock it), and the real cost of building a business. His top posts are almost entirely personal — raw, short, real. No tactics. No frameworks. Just moments.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — Raw life moment (his #1 format)**
```
Last week I sent my mum $10k for her birthday.
```
One sentence. Specific number. No adjective. The emotion comes from the image, not from the words.

**Pattern 2 — Sarcastic AI post**
```
I grabbed a Claude prompt from a LinkedIn automation bro, who built it in 40-50 hours so I didn't have to, and it made me $500k this week.
```
The joke is obvious. No subtlety. Full absurdist satire in one sentence.

**Pattern 3 — Emotional driving moment**
```
I pulled over today and just started crying.

Not bad crying. Good crying.
```
Line 1: happened. Line 2: context. The "not bad crying" line is doing everything.

**Pattern 4 — AI contrast hook**
```
AI made my work faster than I ever thought possible.

Then I watched it fail at the one thing that actually matters.
```
Acknowledges AI value. Then pivots to what it can't do — real life.

**Pattern 5 — Question to the audience**
```
Do people actually fall for this sh*t?
```
Rhetorical. Short. Sarcastic. Image context does the rest.

---

## Body Structure

**Emotional story posts (his most viral):**
- Very short paragraphs — often 1-2 sentences each
- Sometimes single sentences as their own paragraph
- No bullets, no numbered lists, no arrows
- Closes with the real takeaway: one short statement
- "Namaste 🙏" sign-off on many posts

**Sarcastic posts:**
- Hook is the joke
- 2-4 lines that escalate the absurdity
- Punch down on the LinkedIn automation bro culture
- No explanation needed

**Reflection posts:**
- Opens with a real event (driving, closing a deal, waking up)
- Admits the feeling didn't match the milestone
- Ends with what he's doing instead
- Feels like a journal entry, not a LinkedIn post

---

## Voice Markers

- Profanity used naturally: "bullsh*t", "sh*t", "f*ck you money"
- Lowercase when writing about his mother or daughter
- Single sentences as paragraphs — the white space IS the emphasis
- "Namaste 🙏" as a sign-off (his signature)
- Admits weakness without performing it: "I feel weird sharing the numbers. Like I haven't earned the right to say them out loud yet."
- No punctuation after short punchy lines: "That's the only reason to get rich."
- Mentions specific songs, people, places: "Bon Iver's 'Heavenly Father'", "Coconut" (his dog)

---

## CTA Style

Almost never asks a question directly. Sometimes:
```
Is it just me? Wanted to share.
```
Or the post just ends:
```
Namaste 🙏
```

When he sells: "ps." — lowercase, after the post. Never part of the post.
```
ps. On April 23rd I'm opening the doors to a small group...
```

---

## What He Never Does

- No tactics or frameworks
- No em dashes
- No "here are 7 things" educational posts
- Never explains his sarcasm
- No motivational closer that wraps everything neatly
- Never sells in the post body — always in the "ps."

---

## When to Use This Style

- Raw personal moments tied to money or family
- Sarcastic takes on LinkedIn culture or AI hype
- "The milestone didn't feel how I expected" posts
- Short emotional observations
