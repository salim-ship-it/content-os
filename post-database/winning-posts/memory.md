## Hook Patterns That Work

Ranked by what drives the most clicks and engagement from the winning posts database.

### Tier 1 — Proven hooks (500+ likes consistently)

| Pattern | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Shocking number + "here's why" | "I receive over 1,000 emails. I replied to just one." | Extreme contrast creates irresistible curiosity |
| Bold contrarian statement | "If your employees are lazy at home, they'll be lazy at the office." | Takes a side. Forces a reaction — agree or fight |
| "Big news" + specific result | "We just acquired Claap in an 8-figure deal." | News + scale = must-read |
| Childhood/past self setup | "When I was a kid, I thought you had to be a badass to win." | Nostalgia + transformation arc hooks emotionally |

### Tier 2 — Reliable hooks (200-500 likes)

| Pattern | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Counterintuitive math | "6x7 is infinitely better than 8x5." | Forces you to stop scrolling and do the math |
| "X is dead, but Y is alive" | "Spammy cold emails are dead. But outbound is still #1." | Validates anxiety then gives hope |
| Metric-led opener | "We run lemlist with a 10X LTV/CAC" | Impressive number = authority |
| "Most people think X. Here's what actually happens." | "As a CEO, I'm almost never a decision maker" | Insider knowledge that challenges assumptions |
| Double standard exposed | "Sales miss quota: fire them. Product miss deadline: adjust." | Hypocrisy everyone feels but nobody says |

### Hook rules
- **First line = the ad for the post.** If it doesn't create tension, curiosity, or surprise — rewrite it.
- **Use numbers.** Posts with specific numbers in the hook outperform vague ones 2-3x.
- **One idea per hook.** Don't try to say two things. Pick the sharpest angle.
- **Contrarian > educational.** "Here's how to X" is weaker than "Everything you know about X is wrong."
- **Short.** Best hooks are under 15 words. The shorter, the punchier.

## CTA Patterns That Work

What drives comments, shares, and DMs at the end of a post.

### High-comment CTAs

| Pattern | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Ask for their version | "What's the best cold email you've ever received?" | People love sharing their own stories |
| Binary choice | "Agree or disagree?" | Low effort to respond. Creates two camps |
| "Comment X to get Y" | "Comment 'playbook' and I'll send it" | Clear exchange — engagement for value |
| Challenge an assumption | "Tell me I'm wrong." | Ego-driven. People rush to prove you wrong |

### High-share CTAs

| Pattern | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| "Tag someone who..." | "Tag a sales leader who needs to see this." | Social proof + relevance |
| "Save this for later" | "Bookmark this if you run outbound." | Signals value. LinkedIn algorithm rewards saves |
| No CTA at all | Let the post speak for itself | Overly salesy CTAs kill authentic posts |

### CTA rules
- **Match the CTA to the post type.** Hot takes → "agree or disagree?" Case studies → "what's yours?" Educational → "save this."
- **Don't ask for engagement on every post.** 2 out of 5 max. The rest should earn it naturally.
- **Never say "like and share."** It's lazy and LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes it.
- **Ask one thing.** "Comment AND share AND follow" = nobody does anything.

## Making Posts Human

What separates high-performing posts from AI-sounding content.

### Do this

| Rule | Example |
|---|---|
| **Start with "I" or a real moment** | "I just watched our PM build a feature in 2 hours." Not "In today's SaaS landscape..." |
| **Name real people** | "Then I met Pierre Bruneau" — names make stories real |
| **Include the messy part** | "Here's what didn't work" — perfection kills trust |
| **Use short sentences** | Break after every thought. One idea per line. Like this. |
| **Write like you talk** | Read it out loud. If it sounds like a blog post, rewrite it. |
| **Show the emotion** | "I was frustrated" > "It was challenging" |
| **Use specific details** | "$35M ARR, +$2M vs August" > "We grew significantly" |
| **Admit what you don't know** | "I have no idea what my kids' life will look like with AI." |

### Never do this

| Anti-pattern | Why it kills the post |
|---|---|
| "In today's fast-paced world..." | Generic AI opener. Instant scroll-past. |
| "Let's dive in" / "Let me break it down" | Filler. Just start the content. |
| Emoji every other line | Looks like a template. 1-2 emojis max. |
| "Key takeaways:" at the end | Blog format. LinkedIn is a conversation. |
| Perfect grammar, zero personality | Reads like ChatGPT. Add imperfections. |
| Every sentence is the same length | Monotone rhythm. Mix short and long. |
| Hashtags at the bottom | Nobody clicks them. They signal "I'm trying too hard." |

### The human test
Before posting, ask: "Would I say this out loud to someone at a bar?" If no, rewrite it.

## Key Insights (Updated per Analysis)

### From Charles Tenot — 50 posts (2026-04-01)

1. **Case studies are the highest-ROI post type.** Highest avg comments (79) and his #1 post overall is a cold email teardown. Always include a real example.
2. **Hot takes have the best comment-to-like ratio (25%).** If you want conversation, write a hot take. If you want reach, write a story.
3. **Stories get 3x more reposts than any other type.** Emotional, personal stories get reshared. Educational content gets saved but not shared.
4. **Every top 10 hook is under 15 words.** Not one exception. Cut your hooks ruthlessly.
5. **8 out of 10 top hooks use a number.** "1000 emails." "$35M ARR." "8-figure deal." Numbers stop the scroll.
6. **Zero top 20 posts use hashtags.** Don't use them.
7. **"I" appears in the first 3 words of 7/10 top posts.** First person = immediate human connection.
8. **Never waste big news on one post.** Charles got 2,465 likes from ONE acquisition by splitting it across 6 posts over 5 weeks.
9. **AI/Claude Code posts are trending up.** His Feb 2026 AI posts outperform his 2025 average. The topic is rising.
10. **He uses zero carousels and zero memes.** His educational posts (avg 243 likes) underperform — carousels could be the unlock for that type.
