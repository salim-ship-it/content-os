# General LinkedIn Rules

Baseline rules for posting, engagement, and growth. Updated as we learn from data.

---

## Posting Rhythm

- **Frequency:** 3-5x per week
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best time:** 7-9am CET (France timezone)
- **Never post:** Saturday, Sunday (unless it's personal/story content)

---

## Content Mix (Weekly)

- 2x educational (how-to, frameworks, tools)
- 1x story (client result, personal lesson)
- 1x hot take or contrarian opinion
- 1x behind the scenes (what you're building, what you shipped)

---

## Engagement Rules

- Comment on 5-10 posts per day from creators in `sources.md`
- Comments must add value — never "great post!" only
- Reply to every comment on your own posts within 2 hours of posting
- DM everyone who comments with a question — start a conversation

---

## Growth Tactics

- **Build in public** — show the process, not just the result
- **Tag people you mention** — they'll engage, their audience sees you
- **First comment** — add context or a question in your own first comment right after posting
- **Post carousels** for how-to content — highest save rate
- **Reshare with insight** — don't just repost, add your take on top

---

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Impressions per post | Reach — is the algorithm pushing it? |
| Like rate (likes / impressions) | Resonance — did people care? |
| Comment rate | Engagement depth — did it spark conversation? |
| Save rate | Value — did people want to come back to it? |
| Follower growth per week | Momentum — are you attracting the right people? |
| Inbound DMs from posts | Conversion — is content generating leads? |

---

## Writing Rules — Sound Human

- **Never use the "if not X, it's Y" pattern.** Lines like "It's not the prompt. It's the data." or "They're not the ones with X. They're the ones with Y." sound like LinkedIn ghostwriter clichés. Read like AI slop.
- **No symmetrical contrasts stacked back to back.** One is fine if it lands. Three in a row is a tell.
- **Write like you're explaining something at a coffee.** Loose rhythm, one idea per paragraph, an aside or a small specific detail every few lines.
- **Specific beats abstract.** "Two weeks pulling call transcripts" beats "the data infrastructure underneath." Numbers, names, time periods.
- **End on something earned, not declared.** Don't summarize the post in two parallel sentences. Land somewhere quieter.
