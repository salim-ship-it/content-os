# Viral Posts Rules

What makes a text-only LinkedIn post go viral (500+ likes). Every rule here comes from analyzing the winning posts database.

---

## Anatomy of a Viral Post

```
[Hook — 1 line, punchy, creates tension or curiosity]

[Space]

[Setup — 2-3 lines of context. Short sentences.]

[Space]

[Core content — the insight, story, or argument. Use line breaks.]

[Space]

[CTA or closing line — optional, depends on post type]
```

---

## What the Data Says

From analyzing the top 50 posts by Charles Tenot and other creators:

### Posts with 1000+ likes share these traits:
- Hook is under 15 words
- First line creates an extreme contrast ("1000 emails, replied to 1")
- Content reveals something most people don't see (behind the scenes, insider view)
- Mix of emotion + data — not just one or the other
- No hashtags, no emojis in hook, minimal formatting

### Posts with 500-1000 likes share these traits:
- Strong contrarian angle OR personal vulnerability
- One clear idea, not multiple
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each)
- End with a natural CTA or question, not forced

### Posts with 200-500 likes share these traits:
- Educational with clear structure (numbered lists, bullet points)
- Specific numbers or metrics in the hook
- "How we did X" format works consistently
- Carousels and visual posts tend to cluster here

## Post Structure Rules

1. **First line is everything.** If it doesn't create tension, curiosity, or surprise — kill it and start over.
2. **One idea per post.** Never try to say two things.
3. **Short sentences.** Break after every thought. Like this. Read it out loud.
4. **White space is your friend.** Dense paragraphs die on LinkedIn. Use line breaks aggressively.
5. **Lead with the payoff, not the setup.** Don't bury the insight after 5 paragraphs of context.
6. **Specific > vague.** "$35M ARR" beats "we grew significantly."
7. **Show the messy part.** "Here's what didn't work" builds more trust than a highlight reel.
8. **No hashtags.** Nobody clicks them. They signal "I'm trying too hard."
9. **Emojis: 0-2 max.** Used sparingly for emphasis, never as bullet points.

---

## CTA Rules for Viral Posts

| Post type | Best CTA | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hot take | Ask for debate | "Agree or disagree?" |
| Case study | Ask for theirs | "What's the best cold email you've ever received?" |
| Educational | Encourage saves | "Bookmark this if you run outbound." |
| Story | No CTA | Let it land. Silence is powerful. |
| Transparency | Invite questions | "What would you want me to break down next?" |

**Max 2 out of 5 posts should have an explicit CTA.** The rest should earn engagement naturally.

1. **Short → long → short rhythm.** First line is punchy. Middle paragraphs expand. Last line is punchy again. Never uniform paragraph length.

### Anti-AI Rules (added 2026-04-08, after Vibe GTM post audit)

The biggest reason a post underperforms isn't the topic or the hook — it's that it sounds AI-written. Even strong takes flop when the prose has the smell. These are the patterns to avoid every single time.

**Banned patterns (every one of these is a tell):**

1. **"It's not X. It's Y." reveal.** Example: "The problem isn't the prompt. It's what's behind the prompt." Every LinkedIn ghostwriter uses this. One per post MAX, and only if it's truly earned.
2. **Rhetorical questions as transitions.** Example: "The people winning right now? They're the ones who…" It's a fake conversation. Make the point without staging it.
3. **Symmetrical sentence pairs stacked back to back.** Example: "The prompt is 10%. The data is 90%." Once is fine. Twice in a row is a tell. Three times = obviously AI.
4. **Parallel bullet lists.** When every bullet starts with the same word or has the same length, it reads as machine-generated. Break the rhythm. Vary one bullet.
5. **Em dash addiction.** AI uses em dashes for everything. Max one per post. Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.
6. **Closing with a moral or summary.** Don't wrap the post in a bow. Real posts often end on a small specific moment, a question, or just stop.
7. **Filler phrases.** "the truth is", "here's the thing", "let me be clear", "the reality is", "in fact", "essentially", "fundamentally". Cut on sight.
8. **AI vocabulary tells.** "leverage", "unlock", "harness", "navigate", "elevate", "delve", "robust", "tapestry", "landscape", "ecosystem", "in the realm of", "seamless". Nobody talks like this.

**What to do instead:**

- Open with a fact, a number, or a moment that actually happened.
- Use uneven sentence rhythm. Short, then medium, then short again. Never uniform.
- Drop one specific detail no AI would invent (a real name, a real number, a real time period).
- End on something quieter than the hook. The best closers sound like the writer ran out of things to say and walked away.
- Cut before you polish. Most AI-sounding posts get fixed by deletion, not rewriting.

**Honesty rule:**

Never invent stats. If you don't have data, reframe as personal observation. "7 out of 10 Vibe GTM emails I get sound like bots" is honest and fact-checkable (it's your inbox). "70% of marketers say…" is a fake stat.

### Storytelling Rule (added 2026-04-08)

The single biggest gap in opinion posts is missing a personal moment. A post made entirely of opinions scores 3/10 on storytelling no matter how sharp the take is. Adding ONE specific thing you actually did — with a number or a time period — pushes the same post from a 32/60 to a ~46/60.

**Template for the personal moment:**
> Last [specific time period], I [specific action with a number]. [What happened next, with a result].

**Example:**
> Last month I spent two weeks tagging Fireflies transcripts before I wrote a single prompt. Reply rate on that campaign was 3x my previous one.

Drop it before the closing line. It changes the post from opinion to proof.

### Audit-Driven Writing (added 2026-04-08)

Every draft should be audited before it goes out. Use `/skills/post-scorer/SKILL.md` to score on six dimensions:

1. **AI Smell** (lower = better) — does it pass the anti-AI patterns above?
2. **Hook** — first line under 10 words, specific, no warmup
3. **CTA** — one specific question that drives comments (not "what do you think?")
4. **Format** — white space, single-sentence paragraphs, max 2 emojis
5. **Structure** — one clear idea, hook → evidence → landing
6. **Storytelling** — at least one specific personal moment

A post under 40/60 is not ready. Most rewrites fix two dimensions at once: storytelling + CTA.
