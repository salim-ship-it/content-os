# Jack J — Creator Style Guide

> 12 viral posts analyzed. Lead gen results. Comment-driven CTA. Insane engagement (1780 comments peak).

---

## Who He Is

Lead generation operator. Posts about booking meetings, outbound results, and free systems for SDRs. His posts generate 1000+ comments regularly — almost entirely because of a single CTA mechanic: "Comment [WORD] to get X." His content is result-first, proof-driven, and built around giveaways.

---

## Hook Patterns (real examples from his top posts)

**Pattern 1 — Team result with specific numbers**
```
My team has booked 847 meetings in 90 days.
```
Specific number + timeframe. Not "hundreds of meetings" — 847. The precision is the proof.

**Pattern 2 — Specific result without preamble**
```
We've sent 12,400 cold emails this month.

Here's everything we learned.
```
Volume claim. Then what it unlocks.

**Pattern 3 — Free system announcement**
```
I built a free cold email system that books meetings on autopilot.

Comment "SYSTEM" to get it.
```
Built + result + CTA. Three lines, three jobs done.

**Pattern 4 — The "not X, a real Y" structure**
```
Not a course. Not a template. A live working campaign.
```
Three-part triple that strips the thing down to what it actually is.

**Comment-driven giveaway post:**
```

```

**Results breakdown post:**
```
[Hook: volume or outcome]

[2-3 lines of what they learned or did]

→ Key insight 1
→ Key insight 2
→ Key insight 3

[What to do with this insight]
```

---

## Voice Markers

- → arrows for all lists — never bullets or ✦
- "Not a course. Not a [thing]. A [real thing]." — signature triple structure
- Specific numbers every time: "847 meetings", "12,400 emails", "90 days"
- Short declarative sentences — no transitions like "As a result..." or "Therefore..."
- "Comment [WORD]" always in quotes, always ALL CAPS word
- No personal backstory — goes straight to result

---

## CTA Style

One dominant CTA pattern:

**Comment trigger (his standard):**
```
Comment "MEETINGS" and I'll DM you the exact system we used.
```

The word is always:
- Related to the resource (MEETINGS, SYSTEM, EMAILS, PLAYBOOK)
- In quotes
- ALL CAPS
- Followed by "and I'll DM you" not "and I'll send you"

---

## What He Never Does

- No emotional or personal stories
- Never uses em dashes
- No motivational wrap-up at the end
- Doesn't explain his methodology in depth — just shares results and the system
- No "thoughts?" or open questions — always a specific action CTA

---

## When to Use This Style

- Lead magnet giveaway posts (highest engagement format)
- "Here's what we learned from X" breakdown posts
- Results-first posts where the stat is the proof
- Any post where the CTA is "comment to get something"
